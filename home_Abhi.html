About us

We are passionate about understanding how cells move, sense, and adapt. Our lab investigates how the cytoskeleton enables cell migration and adaptation to biochemical and biophysical cues in the environment. We combine hypothesis-driven and curiosity-driven approaches, using synthetic cell biology to engineer chemigenetic and optogenetic molecular actuators that probe and reconstitute complex signaling networks.

Our group works in an open, multidisciplinary research space that fosters collaboration across biology, physics, and engineering. We welcome students and postdocs who are excited to learn diverse experimental techniques, develop innovative molecular tools, and tackle fundamental questions in cell biology. If you’re passionate about discovery and creative problem-solving, we’d love to hear from you to discuss potential research opportunities.

Our lab investigates how cells use their cytoskeleton to sense and adapt to their environments. We study how the cytoskeleton responds to biochemical and biophysical cues to initiate molecular signaling pathways to effect various cellular responses. Using synthetic cell biology approaches, we develop chemigenetic and optogenetic molecular actuators to dissect and reconstitute complex signaling networks that regulate these processes. Our research takes place in an open, multidisciplinary lab environment that encourages collaboration across biology, physics, and engineering. We welcome students and postdocs who are eager to learn diverse techniques, create innovative molecular tools, and address fundamental questions in cell biology. If you are passionate about discovery and interdisciplinary science, we invite you to reach out to discuss potential research opportunities.
