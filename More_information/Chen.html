Chen-Hsiang

Chen-Hsiang Hsu is a Master’s student in the Professional Science Master’s program in Applied Genomics at the University of Connecticut, currently conducting research at the Center for Cell Analysis and Modeling (CCAM) at UConn Health. Her work focuses on in vitro modeling using 3D mammalian cell culture systems, cell migration and collagen invasion assays, as well as imaging-based analysis through fluorescence and phase-contrast microscopy.

She brings prior industry experience from her role as a Quality Assurance Specialist at ACT Genomics in Taipei, where she supported regulatory compliance and quality systems aligned with ISO 13485, ISO 27001, and CAP standards.

Chen-Hsiang holds a B.S. in Food Science and Biotechnology from National Chung Hsing University in Taiwan, where she also served as a research assistant studying the bioactivity of organic and conventional produce using spectroscopy and statistical modeling. Her technical skill set spans molecular biology, cell biology, and statistical analysis tools including R, SAS EG, and GraphPad Prism.
